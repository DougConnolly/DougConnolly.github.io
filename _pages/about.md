I am a mathematician currently working as a postdoc in the Quantum Architecture Unit at the Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology (OIST) in Okinawa Japan. Most recently I have been interested in applications based on graph theory, and have worked on projects involving resource optimization for quantum communication.

Prior to joining OIST, I worked as a postdoc at Inria, the National Institute for Research in Digital Science and Technology, in Paris, France (2021 to August 2023). My project was related to quantum error correction.

I received a PhD in mathematics from the University of Iowa in 2021. My research focused on knot theory and the tabulation of 2-string tangles.

I have been studying Japanese for more than five years (which includes 6 months living with host families in Japan) and speak conversational Japanese. I am actively working toward achieving JLPT N2 certification.

For several years now I have lived and worked abroad with international research teams. Working in a multicultural environment has been a joy for me and something I intend to continue.

When not reflecting on the intricacies of graph theory, I enjoy travel, hiking, and scuba diving. Japan has some of the most beautiful reefs I have ever seen.
